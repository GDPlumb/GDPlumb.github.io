Resume

I am a fifth-year PhD student in Carnegie Mellon Univiersity's Machine Learning Department advised by Ameet Talwalkar. My research focuses on the theory and applications of explainable machine learning, with a focus on understanding powerful black-box models such as neural networks and random forests.

Recent Updates

  • Jan 2022: Our white paper on the need to ground methods from interpretable machine learning in real use cases was published at ACM Queue.
  • December 2021: Our paper on simulating user studies for the evaluation of explanation methods was presented at the eXplainable AI approaches for debugging and diagnosis workshop at NeurIPS.
  • June 2021: We released a paper for identifying and mitigating spurious patterns in image classifiers using counterfactual images.
  • May 2021: We released a benchmark for evaluating the quality of saliency map explanations. Interestingly, we found that existing explanation methods do not perform well unless the model's reasoning is very simple.
