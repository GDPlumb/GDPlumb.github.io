Resume

I am a second-year PhD student in Carnegie Mellon Univiersity's Machine Learning Department advised by Ameet Talwalkar. My research focuses on the theory and applications of explainable machine learning, with a focus on understanding powerful black-box models such as neural networks and random forests.

Recent Updates

  • January 2019: Submited ExpO, a paper on regularizing black-box models to be more amenable to local explanation, to ICLM 2019.
  • December 2018: Published MAPLE, a paper on producing better local explanations for black-box models using tree ensembles, at NeurIPS 2018.
